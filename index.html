Homelessness in the Pacific Northwest

Understanding Portland & Vancouver

Header image courtesy of Unsplash

Artist’s Statement

This project explores homelessness as both a social and human issue, focusing on its visibility in the Pacific Northwest, particularly in cities like Portland and Vancouver. The goal of this website is not only to present information, but to challenge the viewer’s perception of homelessness beyond statistics and stereotypes. Too often, homelessness is viewed as a distant or abstract problem, when in reality it exists in everyday spaces and affects real individuals with complex stories.



Through the use of two media objects, this project combines visual data with emotional storytelling. The infographic presents key facts, causes, and potential solutions in a clear and accessible format, allowing viewers to understand the scale and complexity of the issue. In contrast, the video remix emphasizes the human experience by using real-world footage to highlight the emotional realities of homelessness. Together, these elements create a balance between information and empathy.



The design of the website reinforces this message through its visual tone and layout. Darker colors and strong contrasts reflect the seriousness of the issue, while the structured navigation encourages viewers to explore each component of the project. Ultimately, this work aims to raise awareness, encourage reflection, and promote a deeper understanding of homelessness as a societal issue that requires both attention and action.

Knull Ackerman | 2026
